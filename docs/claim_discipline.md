# Claim Discipline

Phase 7 tightens OHIE language so that claims stay inside the physics actually implemented.

## Required Renames

| Old term | Preferred term | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Compound flooding | Compound forcing scaffold | Until flux-based coupling matures into a defensible coupled formulation |
| Validation | Benchmark reproduction scaffold | Unless the case is calibrated against observations and documented as such |
| Intervention intelligence | Intervention comparison framework | Until search, optimization, and ranking are actually implemented |
| Real-data validation | Real-terrain sanity testing | Unless the comparison uses real observation alignment with documented preprocessing |

## Claim Rules

- Do not imply calibrated realism without calibration.
- Do not imply compound river-urban coupling when the boundary is still an exchange approximation.
- Do not imply ranking or optimization when only scenario comparison exists.
- Do not call proxy comparisons “remote sensing validation.”

## Safe Phrasing

Use:

- “flux-coupled boundary approximation”
- “benchmark reproduction scaffold”
- “real-terrain sanity test”
- “intervention comparison framework”
- “proxy comparison only”

Avoid:

- “validated”
- “operational”
- “calibrated” unless the evidence is explicit
- “decision intelligence” unless the system can search and rank interventions
