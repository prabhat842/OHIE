# Phase 7R Reviewer Attacks

## Skeptical Hydrologist

Strongest remaining criticism:

- The flux coefficient is now explainable, but it is still an empirical conductance on a small local terrain chip, not a calibrated river-plain law.

Confidence increase:

- The sensitivity curve now shows a defensible operating region and clear tradeoffs between responsiveness and mass error.

Unresolved concern:

- No measured hydrograph, no basin-specific calibration, and no transferability proof across terrains.

## Flood Modeler

Strongest remaining criticism:

- The boundary benchmark is qualitative; it shows the direction of response, not hydraulic truth.

Confidence increase:

- The stage-response benchmark and coefficient sweep make the boundary approximation easier to reason about and easier to bound.

Unresolved concern:

- The model still relies on diffusive-wave physics and simplified exchange laws.

## Municipal Engineer

Strongest remaining criticism:

- The language freeze is good, but the system still cannot rank or optimize interventions.

Confidence increase:

- The public wording now stays inside the implemented physics more reliably.

Unresolved concern:

- No design-grade infrastructure sizing, no budget-constrained choice support.

## Reproducibility Reviewer

Strongest remaining criticism:

- The new boundary benchmarks are reproducible, but they still use a local derived terrain chip.

Confidence increase:

- The benchmark and sensitivity scripts now make the operating region explicit and rerunnable.

Unresolved concern:

- Still no fully open-data-native river boundary benchmark.
