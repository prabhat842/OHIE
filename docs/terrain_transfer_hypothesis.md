# Terrain Transfer Hypothesis

The current flux-coupling approximation appears to transfer best in:

- broad low-gradient floodplain terrain
- basin-like low-relief terrains
- river-valley terrain with moderate relief but limited heterogeneity

It weakens in:

- mixed-relief terrain
- regimes with high relief variance, high roughness, or narrow storage geometry

In the current sweep, steep terrain did not fail outright, but mixed relief was the clearest weak-transfer regime.

This is a reviewer-defensible hypothesis based on the current regime sweep.

It is not a law, not calibration, and not a claim of universal transferability.
