# Failure Classification

Current read from the Dhakuakhana open-DEM case:

- local failure on the internal benchmark family
- partial transfer on the external open-DEM floodplain
- no evidence yet for a universal default

## Outcome A: Local Failure

Meaning:

- the external open-DEM case improves relative to the benchmark family
- the coefficient is still terrain dependent, but the failure is local to the original benchmark family

Implication:

- a bounded empirical approximation may still be possible

## Outcome B: Structural Failure

Meaning:

- the coefficient still shows strong terrain dependence or high mass error in the external open-DEM case

Implication:

- the current coefficient should be treated as benchmark-local only
- a terrain-aware empirical law may be needed later

## Outcome C: Partial Transfer

Meaning:

- the approximation responds outside the original benchmark family
- but the operating region remains narrow and terrain regime still matters

Implication:

- the method is not universal, but it is more than a one-off artifact
