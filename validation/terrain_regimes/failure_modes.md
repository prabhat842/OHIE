# Failure Modes

## Steep terrain overreaction

Expected failure:

- the flux-coupling coefficient stops behaving like a bounded empirical approximation in steep terrain

Why:

- strong slope can dominate local exchange behavior

Confidence implication:

- weak transfer on steep terrain is expected and does not invalidate the whole method

## Mixed-relief instability

Expected failure:

- transfer becomes inconsistent when slope and storage vary sharply within the tile

Why:

- a single coefficient cannot represent strongly heterogeneous terrain everywhere

Confidence implication:

- a mixed-relief failure supports the idea of terrain-regime dependence

## Basin-storage ambiguity

Expected failure:

- persistence may remain high while boundary exchange still looks weak or uncertain

Why:

- storage geometry can sustain ponding without proving transferable boundary behavior

Confidence implication:

- persistence alone is not enough to claim strong transfer

## Floodplain under-response

Expected failure:

- very low-gradient terrain may show a visible but still limited response

Why:

- the coefficient is only a conductance approximation

Confidence implication:

- under-response suggests the approximation is bounded, not universal
