# Patterns

The current fixed-coefficient study suggests that transfer is most stable in broad low-gradient terrains, but still only at a moderate level under the current criterion.

Observed tendencies:

- flat floodplain, moderate river valley, basin-storage, and steep-edge cases all remain in the moderate-transfer band
- mixed relief is the only case that clearly drops into weak transfer
- the mixed-relief tile also has the highest mean slope, slope variance, relief range, and roughness

Simple reading:

- transfer weakens as relief heterogeneity increases
- the coefficient remains usable in low-gradient and basin-like terrains, but it is not universal
- steepness alone is not enough to break transfer in this sweep; mixed relief is the sharper failure mode

This is a hypothesis from a small regime sweep, not a law and not calibration evidence.
